Capital themes

Where the checks clustered in Q1

AI for robotics ~ $730M across three names — Physical Intelligence ($280M), Skild AI ($250M), Covariant ($200M). Thesis: software / foundation models capture outsized value as hardware commoditizes.

Humanoids ~ $700M — Apptronik $350M Series C the largest. Pace cooling slightly as investors wait for commercial deployment data.

Warehouse / logistics ~ $450M across 18 deals — the most mature category, mostly growth-stage capital.

Ag robotics ~ $280M + medical robotics ~ $180M — both sectors transitioning from "interesting" to "commercially validated".

Why this matters for Auki. Every category above — humanoids, warehouse fleets, AR + robotics co-deployments, embodied AI — is multi-vendor and multi-device by default. Each stack carries its own map truth, its own permissions, its own update cadence. As autonomy frequency rises, coordination errors get expensive fast. Auki / Posemesh is the shared spatial coordination layer — canonical spatial graph, tenancy & permissions, live updates — that lets these funded fleets actually compose at a site.

Investor signal

Strategics are leaning in

Corporate venture capital participated in ~40%+ of Q1 deal dollar volume — NVIDIA, Google Ventures, Amazon, Microsoft, plus OEM strategics (Mercedes-Benz, BMW, Foxconn). Signal: appetite for production paths, not lab demos alone.

Pipeline

Seed bench is healthy

34 seed deals in Q1 with average size up to ~$3.8M (vs ~$2.9M YoY). Robotics remains capital-intensive vs pure software — a structural reason coordination infrastructure (shared maps, shared tenancy) will be bought, not rebuilt.
