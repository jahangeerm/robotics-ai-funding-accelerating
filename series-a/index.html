C · Category validation

Spatial coordination is becoming an investible category.

Two years ago, "shared spatial layer" was a pitch deck bullet. In 2025–26 it became a category with scaled competitors, growth-stage strategics, and quantifiable TAM.

Adjacent TAM

Digital twin: $29B → $223B

Digital twin market expected to grow from $29.3B in 2025 to $223.6B by 2034 at a 25.3% CAGR — a direct adjacency for a shared spatial graph with tenancy & updates.

Spatial computing broader market tracked at $169B – $852B in 2025 depending on definition; forecast CAGRs of 18–21% to 2034. Note: estimates vary widely — use narrowly when quoted.

Why this matters Niantic Spatial is taking the consumer-AR slice. Auki's positioning — decentralized, permissioned, multi-vendor, tenant-aware — is what enterprise and robotics fleets actually need and what a Niantic-style closed platform cannot deliver. The question isn't whether the category exists. It's whether Auki captures the enterprise/robotics half before it consolidates.

E · The Auki case to underwrite

What Nils needs to greenlight.

Market conditions above are necessary but not sufficient. Below is the specific Auki case — editable. Fill each line with the strongest current truth; the story only needs to be defensible, not perfect.

  • Traction [ pilots signed / venues live / robots + AR clients integrated / paying customers / ARR band ]
  • Commercial proof [ signed LOIs · top 3 target logos · revenue in next 2 quarters ]
  • Moat Decentralized, permissioned, multi-vendor-native spatial coordination layer. Not replaceable by a closed Niantic-style map. Network effects compound as vendors onboard.
  • IP & technical [ patents filed · SDK maturity · supported device classes · live map coverage · uptime SLA ]
  • Team & additions [ key hires locked or in pipeline · notable advisors / board candidates ]
  • Use of funds [ runway to Series B · hires split (eng / commercial / GTM) · infra spend · marketing to developer community ]
  • Round ask [ target raise $ / target pre-money $ / lead-investor profile / preferred closing month / reserve for strategics ]
  • Risk to call out [ the honest thing we'd rather an investor hear from us first ]
Decision Greenlighting means: (1) agree the window thesis above, (2) confirm which placeholders are ready to fill today vs. need 4–6 weeks of prep, and (3) authorize work on deck + data room this month, targeting first meetings in early Q3 2026.
